| 10 | **Conquest of Persia** | 633-654 CE | Arab Muslim forces vs. Sasanian Empire | Complete Muslim conquest [Established] | The fall of a thousand-year civilization. Province by province. Ctesiphon's treasures. Yazdegerd's flight and murder at a miller's hands. Cultural transformation. | [S] |

## The Fitna Civil Wars (656-692 CE)

| # | Conflict | Date | Belligerents | Outcome | Fiction Potential | Status |
|---|----------|------|--------------|---------|-------------------|--------|
| 11 | **First Fitna (First Islamic Civil War)** | 656-661 CE | Ali's supporters vs. multiple factions (Aisha/Talha/Zubayr, then Muawiya) | Muawiya becomes caliph; Umayyad dynasty founded [Established] | Shakespearean political drama. Includes Battle of the Camel (Aisha leads troops), Battle of Siffin (arbitration trick), and Ali's assassination by a Kharijite. Birth of Sunni-Shia divide. | [S] |
| 12 | **Battle of the Camel** | 656 CE | Ali vs. Aisha, Talha, and Zubayr | Ali's victory [Established] | Aisha rides into battle in a howdah on a camel. Former companions fight each other. Talha and Zubayr killed. First Muslim-on-Muslim pitched battle. | [S] |
| 13 | **Battle of Siffin** | 657 CE | Ali vs. Muawiya | Inconclusive; led to arbitration [Established] | Qurans on spears -- Muawiya's soldiers raise Qurans to demand arbitration, outmaneuvering Ali. Origin of the Kharijite movement (those who rejected arbitration). | [S] |
| 14 | **Battle of Karbala** | 680 CE (10 Muharram 61 AH) | Husayn ibn Ali and ~72 followers vs. Umayyad army (~4,000+) | Umayyad victory; Husayn and followers killed [Established] | The most emotionally resonant event in Shia Islam. Grandson of the Prophet, with women and children, facing impossible odds. Thirst, betrayal, massacre. Commemorated annually during Muharram (*Ashura*). Parallels to the Passion narrative. | [S] |
| 15 | **Second Fitna** | 680-692 CE | Multiple claimants: Umayyads, Ibn al-Zubayr (Mecca), various rebels | Umayyad victory under Abd al-Malik [Established] | Siege of Mecca (Ka'ba damaged by catapults). Ibn al-Zubayr's counter-caliphate. Mukhtar's revolt in Kufa. Empire nearly collapses. | [S] |

| 19 | **Battle of Tours/Poitiers** | 732 CE | Frankish forces under Charles Martel vs. Muslim raiding force under Abd al-Rahman al-Ghafiqi | Frankish victory [Established] | Western tradition: "saved Christendom."
