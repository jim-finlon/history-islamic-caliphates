## The Prophet & His Companions (610-661 CE)

| # | Figure | Dates | Role | Fiction Potential | Status |
|---|--------|-------|------|-------------------|--------|
| 1 | **Muhammad** (the Prophet) | c. 570-632 CE | Prophet of Islam; founder of the Muslim community and Medinan state | Central figure of any early Islamic narrative. Merchant-turned-prophet; military commander; statesman. Must be treated with particular scholarly care. | [S] |
| 2 | **Abu Bakr al-Siddiq** | c. 573-634 CE | First Rashidun caliph; Muhammad's closest companion and father-in-law | Gentle elder thrust into leadership. Ridda Wars -- holding the new state together after the Prophet's death. | [S] |
| 3 | **Umar ibn al-Khattab** | c. 584-644 CE | Second Rashidun caliph; architect of the conquests and early Islamic administration | Towering personality. Converted from enemy to champion. Created the institutional framework of the caliphate. Assassinated. | [S] |
| 4 | **Uthman ibn Affan** | c. 576-656 CE | Third Rashidun caliph; compiled the Quran | Wealthy Meccan aristocrat. His murder triggered the First Fitna. Tragic arc from respected elder to besieged caliph. | [S] |
| 5 | **Ali ibn Abi Talib** | c. 601-661 CE | Fourth Rashidun caliph; cousin and son-in-law of Muhammad; first Shia imam | Warrior, scholar, judge. Central to the Sunni-Shia divide. Tragic figure -- revered but politically outmaneuvered. Assassinated. | [S] |
| 6 | **Khadija bint Khuwaylid** | c. 555-619 CE | First wife of Muhammad; first Muslim convert; wealthy merchant | Older, wealthy woman who proposed to younger Muhammad. Supported his mission emotionally and financially. Remarkable figure. | [S] |
| 7 | **Aisha bint Abi Bakr** | c. 613/614-678 CE | Wife of Muhammad; political figure; transmitter of hadith | Brilliant, outspoken, politically active. Led an army at the Battle of the Camel. Major hadith source. Controversial figure between Sunni/Shia traditions. | [S] |
| 8 | **Fatimah bint Muhammad** | c. 605-632 CE | Daughter of Muhammad; wife of Ali; mother of Husayn and Hasan | Revered in both traditions but especially in Shia Islam. Her grief and contested inheritance are key narrative elements. | [S] |
| 9 | **Khalid ibn al-Walid** | d. 642 CE | Greatest early Muslim military commander; "Sword of God" (*Sayf Allah*) | Undefeated general. Former enemy who converted. Key victories at Yarmouk, conquest of Iraq. Dismissed by Umar -- loyalty vs. glory tension. | [S] |
| 10 | **Amr ibn al-As** | c. 573-664 CE | Conqueror of Egypt; governor; political ally of Muawiya | Shrewd, worldly diplomat-warrior. Conquered Egypt. Supported Muawiya against Ali. Master of realpolitik. | [S] |
| 11 | **Abu Sufyan ibn Harb** | c. 565-653 CE | Meccan leader; opponent then late convert to Islam; father of Muawiya | From arch-enemy to reluctant Muslim. Aristocratic pride. Dynasty founder through his son. | [S] |
| 12 | **Bilal ibn Rabah** | c. 580-640 CE | Ethiopian former slave; first muezzin; companion of the Prophet | Symbol of Islam's egalitarian ideal. Freed from slavery. His voice called the faithful to prayer. Emotional resonance. | [S] |

## Umayyad Period (661-750 CE)

| # | Figure | Dates | Role | Fiction Potential | Status |
|---|--------|-------|------|-------------------|--------|
| 13 | **Muawiya I ibn Abi Sufyan** | c. 602-680 CE | Founder and first caliph of the Umayyad dynasty | Political genius. Transformed caliphate from elected to hereditary. Master of *hilm* (patient cunning). Damascus court. | [S] |
| 14 | **Husayn ibn Ali** | 626-680 CE | Grandson of Muhammad; martyred at Karbala; central Shia figure | The tragedy of Karbala. Small band against overwhelming odds. Martyrdom that shaped a civilization. Passion play (*ta'ziya*). | [S] |
| 15 | **Yazid I ibn Muawiya** | c. 645-683 CE | Second Umayyad caliph; responsible for Karbala | Villain of the Karbala narrative (in Shia tradition). Wine-loving, poetry-reciting prince. Complex reality vs. demonized image. | [S] |
| 16 | **Abd al-Malik ibn Marwan** | 646-705 CE | Fifth Umayyad caliph; builder of the Dome of the Rock; Arabizer of the state | Empire-builder. Made Arabic the language of administration. Built the Dome of the Rock. Crushed Second Fitna. | [S] |
| 17 | **Al-Hajjaj ibn Yusuf** | 661-714 CE | Umayyad governor of Iraq; ruthless enforcer; patron of Arabic language | Iron-fisted governor. Feared and hated. Built Wasit. Standardized Arabic script. "I see heads ripe for the picking." | [S] |
| 18 | **Umar ibn Abd al-Aziz (Umar II)** | c. 682-720 CE | Umayyad caliph; attempted religious reform; "the fifth Rashidun caliph" | Reformist among worldly Umayyads. Tried to return to early Islamic ideals. Possibly poisoned by his own family. | [S] |
| 19 | **Tariq ibn Ziyad** | d. c. 720 CE | Berber commander; conqueror of Iberia (711 CE) | Burned his boats (possibly legendary). Former slave who conquered a peninsula. Gibraltar = Jabal Tariq. | [S] |
| 20 | **Musa ibn Nusayr** | c. 640-716 CE | Arab governor of North Africa; directed the conquest of Iberia | Tariq's superior. Jealous of Tariq's glory? Recalled and disgraced. Cautionary tale of imperial politics. | [S] |
| 21 | **Mukhtar al-Thaqafi** | c. 622-687 CE | Revolutionary leader in Kufa; champion of Ali's family | Charismatic rebel. Claimed divine inspiration. Used proto-propaganda. Rose and fell spectacularly. | [S] |

## Abbasid Period (750-1258 CE)

### Caliphs & Court

| # | Figure | Dates | Role | Fiction Potential | Status |
|---|--------|-------|------|-------------------|--------|
| 22 | **Abu al-Abbas al-Saffah** | 722-754 CE | First Abbasid caliph; "the Shedder of Blood" | Revolutionary who overthrew the Umayyads. Massacre of the Umayyad family. Short reign; set the stage. | [S] |
| 23 | **Abu Jafar al-Mansur** | 714-775 CE | Second Abbasid caliph; founder of Baghdad; consolidator of the dynasty | Ruthless, brilliant administrator. Built Baghdad (the Round City). Betrayed and killed Abu Muslim. Penny-pinching tyrant with a vision. | [S] |
| 24 | **Harun al-Rashid** | 763/766-809 CE | Fifth Abbasid caliph; most famous caliph; *Thousand and One Nights* figure | The quintessential caliph. Diplomatic exchanges with Charlemagne. Patronage of arts and learning. Destroyed the Barmakid family. Walks Baghdad in disguise (legendary). | [S] |
| 25 | **Al-Mamun** | 786-833 CE | Seventh Abbasid caliph; patron of the House of Wisdom; theologian-caliph | Scholar-ruler. Founded/expanded the House of Wisdom. Imposed the Mihna (inquisition). Civil war against his brother al-Amin. Complex intellectual. | [S] |
| 26 | **Al-Amin** | 787-813 CE | Sixth Abbasid caliph; deposed and killed by al-Mamun | Harun's favorite son, born to an Arab mother (vs. al-Mamun's Persian mother). Dissolute reputation. Besieged in Baghdad. Murdered. Tragic prince. | [S] |
| 27 | **Al-Mutasim** | 796-842 CE | Eighth Abbasid caliph; creator of the Turkish slave-soldier (mamluk) system | Built Samarra. Introduced Turkish mamluks who would eventually dominate. Sack of Amorion (838). Unintended consequences. | [S] |
| 28 | **Al-Mutawakkil** | 822-861 CE | Tenth Abbasid caliph; reversed the Mihna; persecuted non-Muslims | Ended rationalist theology. Anti-dhimmi measures. Murdered by his own Turkish guards. End of caliphal power. | [S] |

## Scholars, Scientists & Philosophers

| # | Figure | Dates | Role | Fiction Potential | Status |
|---|--------|-------|------|-------------------|--------|
| 35 | **Muhammad ibn Musa al-Khwarizmi** | c. 780-850 CE | Mathematician; father of algebra; worked at the House of Wisdom | Gave us "algebra" (*al-jabr*) and "algorithm" (from his name). Central Asian scholar in Baghdad. Pure intellectual drama. | [S] |
| 36 | **Abu Ali al-Husayn ibn Sina (Avicenna)** | 980-1037 CE | Polymath; physician; philosopher; author of *The Canon of Medicine* | Child prodigy. Court physician to multiple rulers. Wrote on philosophy, medicine, music. Itinerant genius in a turbulent age. | [S] |
| 37 | **Abu al-Walid Muhammad ibn Rushd (Averroes)** | 1126-1198 CE | Philosopher; jurist; Aristotelian commentator; qadi of Cordoba | Defended philosophy against theological attack. Influenced Thomas Aquinas. Exiled by Almohad caliph. Reason vs. faith personified. | [S] |
| 38 | **Abu Rayhan al-Biruni** | 973-1048 CE | Polymath; astronomer; historian; pioneer of comparative religion | Studied India with unprecedented objectivity. Measured the Earth's circumference. Polyglot. Scholarly integrity as drama. | [S] |
| 39 | **Abu Ali al-Hasan ibn al-Haytham (Alhazen)** | c. 965-1040 CE | Father of optics; pioneer of the scientific method | Wrote *Kitab al-Manazir* (Book of Optics). Feigned madness to escape the Fatimid caliph. Experimentalist in an age of theory. | [S] |
| 40 | **Hunayn ibn Ishaq** | 809-873 CE | Nestorian Christian translator; central figure of the translation movement | Christian working in Muslim Baghdad. Translated Greek medical and philosophical texts. Bridge between civilizations. | [S] |
| 41 | **Abu Bakr Muhammad ibn Zakariyya al-Razi (Rhazes)** | c. 854-925 CE | Physician; alchemist; freethinker | Distinguished measles from smallpox. Challenged religious authority. Blinded in old age (by chemicals or punishment -- debated). | [S] |
| 42 | **Al-Kindi** | c. 801-873 CE | First major Islamic philosopher (*faylasuf*); "Philosopher of the Arabs" | Integrated Greek philosophy with Islamic thought. Court philosopher under al-Mamun and al-Mutasim. Library confiscated under al-Mutawakkil. | [S] |
| 43 | **Omar Khayyam** | 1048-1131 CE | Persian poet, mathematician, astronomer | Rubaiyat. Calendar reform. Wine, roses, and cosmic skepticism. Friendship with Hassan-i Sabbah and Nizam al-Mulk (legendary). | [S] |
| 44 | **Al-Ghazali** | 1058-1111 CE | Theologian; philosopher; Sufi; author of *Incoherence of the Philosophers* | Intellectual crisis and spiritual transformation. Left prestigious teaching post to become a wandering Sufi. Synthesis of mysticism and orthodoxy. | [S] |

## Figures for Future Expansion

The following figures merit profiles as research progresses:

### Religious & Legal Scholars
- **Abu Hanifa** (699-767) -- Founder of the Hanafi school of law
- **Malik ibn Anas** (711-795) -- Founder of the Maliki school; *al-Muwatta*
- **Al-Shafi'i** (767-820) -- Founder of the Shafi'i school; systematized Islamic jurisprudence
- **Ahmad ibn Hanbal** (780-855) -- Founder of the Hanbali school; resisted the Mihna
- **Al-Hallaj** (858-922) -- Sufi mystic; executed for saying "I am the Truth" (*Ana al-Haqq*)
- **Rabia al-Adawiyya** (c. 717-801) -- Female Sufi saint; theology of divine love

### Scientists & Physicians
- **Jabir ibn Hayyan** (c. 721-815) -- Father of Arabic alchemy (chemistry)
- **Al-Farabi** (c. 872-950) -- "Second Aristotle"; political philosophy
- **Al-Idrisi** (1100-1165) -- Geographer; created the Tabula Rogeriana for Roger II of Sicily
- **Nasir al-Din al-Tusi** (1201-1274) -- Astronomer; mathematician; served the Mongols
- **Ibn al-Nafis** (1213-1288) -- Discovered pulmonary circulation

### Political & Military
- **Zubayda bint Jafar** (c. 762-831) -- Wife of Harun al-Rashid; patron of public works
- **Al-Khayzuran** (d. 789) -- Former slave; wife of al-Mahdi; mother of Harun; power behind the throne
- **Zenobia** influence: **Sitt al-Mulk** (970-1023) -- Fatimid regent; may have murdered al-Hakim
- **Shajara al-Durr** (d. 1257) -- Only woman to rule Egypt as sultan (briefly); Mamluk transition
